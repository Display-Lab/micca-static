Michigan Collaborative for Contraceptive Access(MICCA)

M2 Provision 60 PP

Measure Name: Provision of postpartum contraception (4 to 60 days)

Measure Description

Percentage of women aged 15-44 years, who had a live birth and were provided a most (sterilization, intrauterine device, implant) or moderately effective (pill, patch, ring, injectable, diaphragm) contraception method within 4 to 60 days postpartum.

 

Rationale

The 4-60 day period reflects ACOG recommendations that women should receive contraceptive care at least within the 6-week postpartum visit

Care Setting

Primary Care and Reproductive Health Settings

 

Scope

Measured on a per woman basis

Data Collection Method

This measure shall be calculated on the basis of data collected from the EMR/Medical Record.

Measure Frequency

This measure is to be reported for every quarter of the measuring year.

Q1        January, February, and March

Q2        April, May, and June

Q3        July, August, and September

Q4        October, November, and December

Measure Calculation
Ratio-based

Numerator Statement

Women aged 15 through 44 who had a live birth during the measuring quarter of the year and were provided a most (sterilization, intrauterine device, implant) or moderately (pill, patch, ring, injectable, diaphragm) effective method of contraception within 4-60 days from the date of delivery.

 

Denominator Statement

Women aged 15 through 44 who had a live birth in the measuring quarter of the year.

Inclusions

Women aged 15-44 years who had a live birth within the hospital setting in that measuring quarter of the year

 

Exclusions

Women who did not have a live birth (i.e., miscarriage, ectopic, stillbirth or induced abortion)

 

Risk Adjustment (for outcome measures)

No risk adjustment or risk stratification

References

 NQF: Perinatal and Reproductive Health 2015-2016 Final Report (http://www.qualityforum.org/Publications/2016/12/Perinatal_and_Reproductive_Health_2015-2016_Final_Report.aspx)
