Michigan Collaborative for Contraceptive Access(MICCA)

M4 LARC 60PP

Measure Name: Provision of Postpartum LARC (4 to 60 days)

 

Measure Description

Percentage of women aged 15-44 years, who had a live birth and were provided a LARC method(IUDs and contraceptive implants) within 4 to 60 days postpartum.

 

Rationale

The 4 to 60 day period reflects ACOG recommendations that women should receive contraceptive care at least within the 6-week postpartum visit.

Care Setting

Primary Care and Reproductive Health Settings

 

Scope

Measured on a per woman basis

Data Collection Method

This measure shall be calculated on the basis of data collected from the EMR/Medical Record.

Measure Frequency

This measure is to be reported for every quarter of the measuring year.

Q1        January, February, and March

Q2        April, May, and June

Q3        July, August, and September

Q4        October, November, and December

 

Measure Calculation

 Ratio-based

Numerator Statement

Women aged 15-44 years who had a live birth during the measuring quarter of the year and were provided a LARC method (IUDs or contraceptive implants) within 4 to 60 days from the day of delivery.

 

Denominator Statement

Women aged 15-44 years who had a live birth during the measuring quarter of the year.

Inclusions

  1. Women aged 15-44 years who had a live birth within the hospital setting in that measuring quarter of the year
  2. Women aged 15-44 years who had a live birth and provided LARC method within 4 to 60 days postpartum within the hospital setting in that measuring quarter of the year

Exclusions

  1. Women who did not have a live birth (i.e., miscarriage, ectopic, stillbirth or induced abortion)
  2. Women aged 15-44 years who had a live birth during the measuring quarter of the year and who were provided LARC after 60 days postpartum

 

Risk Adjustment (for outcome measures)

No risk adjustment or risk stratification

References

 NQF: Perinatal and Reproductive Health 2015-2016 Final Report (http://www.qualityforum.org/Publications/2016/12/Perinatal_and_Reproductive_Health_2015-2016_Final_Report.aspx)
